About Resort

Bousra waterfall

BouSra Waterfall, also known as Bousra or Busra Waterfall, is a popular three-tiered waterfall located in Mondulkiri Province, Cambodia, about 43 kilometers from Sen Monorom, and is a popular destination for tourists and locals.

Sen Monorom waterfall

Sen Monorom Waterfall is located about 2 kilometres from the. provincial capital, Sen Monorom, and is a popular spot for swimming and picnicking. The waterfall is surrounded beautiful forest and is a great place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Mondulkiri.

About Food

Samlor Proung

When people think of Mondulkiri province, of course, the Bousra Waterfall is the first thing that comes to mind. This waterfall is very famous. However, in addition to the beautiful tourist area, I would like to introduce three local dishes: Samlor Proung or also known as Samlor Bok bompung Roussey, Samlor Vi, and Roasted Rat.

Minorities

Punong people

The province is inhabited by the indigenous Punong people (Punong in the spoken language, but according to the natives, they are called Pu-Nong) and some other indigenous people. The Punong food culture can be one of the attractions of this indigenous group who live closely with nature. We can recognize this connection through the food that represents the Punong culture, which is the phrang soup (phrang means bamboo tube).
